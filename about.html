</About Me>

After moving to Oregon at 10 years old, Samuel Ellis (b.2002) fell in love with music through middle school band and playing the bass clarinet. After graduating high school, and a brief gap year due to the pandemic, he would continue his musical endevours at the University of Oregon, pursuing Bachelor's degrees in both Computer Science and Music Technology.

While at U of O, Samuel was able to learn about the intersection of STEM and music, discovering audio programming and data sonfication. Through working with his professors Jeffrey Stolet and Jon Bellona, he was able to develop skills in software such as Max/MSP, Kyma, and Ableton. This resulted in a wide range of projects including New Media sound design, electronic compositions, and the development of data driven instruments. He also highlighted the accessibility implications of music technology. Through working with Michal Young, Samuel was able to aid in adapting pre-existing Python assignments with sonification, providing accessibility to a visually impaired undergraduate student. Samuel has since written a research paper accentuating the benefits of including data sonification in STEM learning.

Currently Samuel works as a Desktop Support Technician, as well as an undergraduate Learning Assistant, providing assistance in a Video Game programming course. As he moves forward in his career, he hopes to continue this technological path. His current interests include working with interactive music systems as well as music programming languages.

After graduation in June of 2025, Samuel is looking forward to starting his new position as a Graduate Assistant at the Peabody Institute at Johns Hopkins, where he will be pursuing a Master’s in Music in Computer Music.
